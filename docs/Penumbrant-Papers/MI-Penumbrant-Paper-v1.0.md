## Executive Summary

This Penumbrant Paper provides a comprehensive analysis of Michigan's cannabis regulatory framework, administered by the Cannabis Regulatory Agency (CRA), formerly under the Department of Licensing and Regulatory Affairs (LARA). Michigan operates both medical (MMFLA) and adult-use (MRTMA) cannabis programs, creating a mature but complex dual-market regulatory environment. The penumbral zones identified in this document address areas where the two programs intersect and where operator obligations meet enforcement discretion.

## 1. Regulatory Authority & Structure

### Primary Regulator
- **Cannabis Regulatory Agency (CRA)** — State of Michigan
- Website: [michigan.gov/cra](https://michigan.gov/cra)

### Statutory Foundation
- **Michigan Regulation and Taxation of Marihuana Act (MRTMA)** — Initiated Law 1 of 2018
- **Medical Marihuana Facilities Licensing Act (MMFLA)** — Public Act 281 of 2016
- **Michigan Medical Marihuana Act (MMMA)** — Initiated Law 1 of 2008
- **Administrative Rules** — R 420.1 et seq.

### Local Authority
- Municipalities retain authority to:
  - Prohibit or limit cannabis establishments
  - Impose additional zoning requirements
  - Require local licenses
  - Set operating restrictions
  - Levy local taxes (up to 3%)

## 2. Licensing Framework

### Adult-Use License Categories (MRTMA)

| License Type | Description | Key Requirements |
|-------------|-------------|------------------|
| **Class A Grower** | Up to 100 plants | Background check, facility security |
| **Class B Grower** | Up to 500 plants | Enhanced security, financial review |
| **Class C Grower** | Up to 2,000 plants | Full compliance infrastructure |
| **Excess Grower** | Unlimited (with approval) | Market condition approval |
| **Processor** | Extraction/manufacturing | HACCP compliance, safety protocols |
| **Retailer** | Point-of-sale | ID verification, inventory tracking |
| **Secure Transporter** | Licensed transport | Vehicle requirements, manifests |
| **Safety Compliance Facility** | Testing laboratory | ISO accreditation, qualified staff |
| **Designated Consumption Establishment** | On-site consumption | Local approval, ventilation |
| **Event Organizer** | Temporary events | Event permits, local approval |

### Medical License Categories (MMFLA)

| License Type | Description | Key Requirements |
|-------------|-------------|------------------|
| **Grower (Class A/B/C)** | Cultivation | Patient supply agreements |
| **Processor** | Manufacturing | Medical product standards |
| **Provisioning Center** | Medical retail | Patient verification |
| **Secure Transporter** | Medical transport | Chain of custody |
| **Safety Compliance Facility** | Medical testing | Medical-grade standards |

### Stacking Allowances
- Single entity may hold multiple license types
- Vertical integration permitted
- Cross-market (medical/adult-use) operation allowed

## 5. Penumbral Zones: Areas of Regulatory Uncertainty

### 5.1 Medical-Adult Use Dual Market Complexity
**Issue:** Operating in both markets creates layered compliance requirements.

**Penumbral Concern:**
- Which regulations apply to which products?
- Inventory tracking across programs
- Pricing differential compliance
- Patient vs. consumer service protocols

**Operator Guidance:** Maintain clear separation of medical and adult-use operations; document all cross-market transfers.

---

### 5.2 Local vs. State Licensing Divergence
**Issue:** Significant variation in local cannabis ordinances across Michigan's 1,800+ municipalities.

**Penumbral Concern:**
- State license approval ≠ local operating permission
- Local requirements may exceed state standards
- Enforcement jurisdiction unclear in some situations
- Local political changes may affect operations

**Operator Guidance:** Conduct detailed local due diligence; maintain relationships with local authorities.

---

### 5.3 Product Safety Testing Variability
**Issue:** Testing laboratory results may vary; no standardized inter-lab validation.

**Penumbral Concern:**
- Different labs may produce different results
- "Lab shopping" concerns without clear prohibition
- Enforcement action triggers uncertain
- Retesting procedures ambiguous

**Operator Guidance:** Establish consistent testing relationships; document lab selection rationale.

---

### 5.4 Packaging Compliance for Novel Products
**Issue:** New product formats may not fit existing packaging rules.

**Penumbral Concern:**
- Pre-rolls, concentrates, infused products have different rules
- "Appeals to minors" standard subjective
- Shape/form restrictions (e.g., gummies resembling animals)
- Marketing vs. packaging line unclear

**Operator Guidance:** Seek CRA guidance before launching novel product formats; document compliance rationale.

---

### 5.5 Social Equity Program Evolution
**Issue:** Michigan's social equity programs creating new compliance categories.

**Penumbral Concern:**
- Qualifying criteria verification
- Ownership maintenance requirements
- Partnership/investor compliance
- Program audit procedures

**Operator Guidance:** Document all social equity qualifications and maintain ownership records meticulously.

---

### 5.6 Caregiver to Commercial Transition
**Issue:** Medical caregivers transitioning to licensed operations.

**Penumbral Concern:**
- Dual status compliance (caregiver + licensee)
- Patient supply limitations
- Inventory source documentation
- Transfer documentation requirements

**Operator Guidance:** Clearly separate caregiver and commercial operations; seek CRA guidance on transition.

## 7. Operator Best Practices

### Documentation Excellence
1. **Maintain real-time METRC compliance** — errors compound quickly
2. **Archive all CRA communications** — minimum 5-year retention
3. **Document corrective actions** — show proactive compliance
4. **Separate medical and adult-use records** — clear audit trail

### Inspection Readiness
1. **Designate compliance point person** for each location
2. **Conduct quarterly internal audits** using CRA standards
3. **Maintain inspection response protocol** with key documents
4. **Train all staff on inspection procedures**

### Multi-Market Management
1. **Clear inventory separation** — medical vs. adult-use
2. **Distinct pricing and point-of-sale** — per program requirements
3. **Staff training on program differences** — patient vs. consumer service
4. **Compliance matrix by program** — track requirements separately

### Regulatory Relationship Management
1. **Attend CRA stakeholder meetings** — documented participation
2. **Request guidance in writing** — create paper trail
3. **Engage local authorities** — municipal relationships matter
4. **Report issues proactively** — before they become enforcement matters
