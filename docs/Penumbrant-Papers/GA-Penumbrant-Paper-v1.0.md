## Executive Summary

This Penumbrant Paper provides a comprehensive analysis of Georgia's limited cannabis regulatory framework. Unlike Colorado, Minnesota, or Michigan, Georgia has NOT legalized adult-use or comprehensive medical cannabis. Georgia operates only a highly restrictive **Low-THC Oil** program under the Georgia Access to Medical Cannabis Commission, governed by HB 1 (Haleigh's Hope Act, 2015) and subsequent amendments. This paper identifies penumbral zones within this narrow regulatory environment where patient and limited operator obligations meet enforcement realities.

---

## ⚠️ CRITICAL NOTICE

**Georgia's cannabis program is fundamentally different from other states:**

- **NO adult-use/recreational cannabis**
- **NO smokable cannabis products permitted**
- **NO edibles permitted**
- **ONLY low-THC oil (≤5% THC, ≥CBD)**
- **LIMITED qualifying conditions**
- **VERY LIMITED licensed dispensaries**

Operators considering Georgia must understand this is a highly restricted medical-only program.

## 1. Regulatory Authority & Structure

### Primary Regulators
- **Georgia Access to Medical Cannabis Commission (GAMCC)** — Program oversight
- **Georgia Department of Public Health (DPH)** — Patient registry
- Website: [dph.georgia.gov](https://dph.georgia.gov)

### Statutory Foundation
- **Haleigh's Hope Act (HB 1, 2015)** — Initial low-THC authorization
- **HB 324 (Hope Act, 2019)** — In-state production authorization
- **O.C.G.A. § 16-12-190 et seq.** — Low-THC Oil Registration
- **Georgia Access to Medical Cannabis Commission Rules**

### Limited Local Authority
- Local governments have limited authority over licensed dispensaries
- Zoning and land use regulations apply
- No authority to expand program beyond state law

## 2. Program Framework

### What is Permitted

| Product Type | Legal Status | Form |
|-------------|--------------|------|
| Low-THC Oil | Legal for registered patients | Tinctures, capsules, lotions, patches, gels |
| Smokable Cannabis | **PROHIBITED** | N/A |
| Edibles | **PROHIBITED** | N/A |
| Vaporizable Products | **PROHIBITED** | N/A |
| Flower/Plant Material | **PROHIBITED** | N/A |

### Product Specifications
- **Maximum THC content:** 5% by weight
- **Minimum CBD content:** Equal to or greater than THC content
- **Possession limit:** 20 fluid ounces of low-THC oil

### Qualifying Medical Conditions
Georgia recognizes approximately 17 specific conditions:

| Condition Category | Examples |
|-------------------|----------|
| Severe/End-Stage | Cancer, ALS, MS |
| Neurological | Parkinson's disease, seizure disorders, autism (specific) |
| Chronic | Crohn's disease, intractable pain |
| Mental Health | PTSD, severe or end-stage conditions |
| Hospice | Any hospice care patient |

**Note:** Conditions can only be added through legislative action, not administrative petition.

## 3. Patient & Operator Compliance Requirements

### Patient Registration Requirements

| Requirement | Standard |
|-------------|----------|
| Physician certification | Georgia-licensed physician |
| Registry application | Submitted through DPH |
| Registration fee | $30 |
| Card validity | 5 years |
| Caregiver registration | Allowed for minors/incapacitated adults |
| Card possession | Must have valid card during possession |

### Licensed Operator Requirements

| License Type | Description | Key Requirements |
|-------------|-------------|------------------|
| **Class 1 Production License** | Cultivation & processing | Limited to 6 licenses statewide |
| **Class 2 Production License** | Smaller scale production | Limited availability |
| **Dispensary License** | Direct patient sales | Associated with production licenses |
| **Independent Pharmacy** | Oil dispensing | Pharmacy license + registration |

### Security & Compliance
- Seed-to-sale tracking system
- Security requirements for production facilities
- Chain of custody documentation
- Testing requirements (potency, contaminants)
- Patient verification systems

## 4. Enforcement Mechanisms

### Law Enforcement Protocols
- DPH maintains 24/7 registry verification hotline
- Online verification system for law enforcement
- Card verification during encounters
- Possession limit enforcement

### Violation Categories

| Violation | Legal Consequences |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Possession without valid card | Criminal charges possible |
| Exceeding possession limit (20 oz) | Criminal charges possible |
| Possession of prohibited forms | Criminal charges (flower, edibles, etc.) |
| Unlicensed production/sale | Serious criminal penalties |
| Diversion to non-registered persons | Serious criminal penalties |

### Criminal Penalties
Georgia's marijuana laws remain strict for non-program activity:
- Possession of marijuana (not low-THC oil) remains criminal
- Unlicensed cultivation is a felony
- Sale outside licensed channels is a felony
- Federal law still applies (no state protection)

## 5. Penumbral Zones: Areas of Regulatory Uncertainty

### 5.1 Dispensary Rollout Delays
**Issue:** Licensed dispensaries have faced significant delays in becoming operational.

**Penumbral Concern:**
- Patients may obtain oil through non-operational licensed channels
- Out-of-state sourcing remains legally gray
- Enforcement during transition period unclear
- Investment planning for operators complicated

**Operator Guidance:** Closely monitor GAMCC announcements; plan for extended timelines.

---

### 5.2 Out-of-State Oil Sourcing
**Issue:** Historically, patients obtained oil from out-of-state due to no in-state production.

**Penumbral Concern:**
- Transportation across state lines violates federal law
- State registry provides only state-level protection
- Enforcement discretion varies by jurisdiction
- In-state availability changing the landscape

**Patient/Operator Guidance:** In-state sourcing now available; transition documentation important.

---

### 5.3 Product Form Interpretation
**Issue:** What constitutes a "gel" vs. an "edible"? When is oil "topical" vs. "ingestible"?

**Penumbral Concern:**
- Product format boundaries not precisely defined
- Innovation limited by form restrictions
- Enforcement may interpret forms differently than operators
- Patient confusion about what is permitted

**Operator Guidance:** Conservative product development; seek GAMCC guidance on novel formats.

---

### 5.4 Federal-State Conflict
**Issue:** Federal law does not recognize Georgia's low-THC oil program.

**Penumbral Concern:**
- Federal employees face employment risk
- Commercial drivers (DOT) face license risk
- Federal property possession prohibited
- Banking access limitations remain

**Operator Guidance:** Clearly communicate federal limitations to patients; plan for banking challenges.

---

### 5.5 Independent Pharmacy Integration
**Issue:** Independent pharmacies authorized to dispense; integration procedures developing.

**Penumbral Concern:**
- Pharmacy compliance requirements layered on cannabis rules
- Inventory tracking across pharmacy and cannabis systems
- Staff training for dual compliance
- Insurance and liability questions

**Operator Guidance:** Pharmacy operators should engage both Board of Pharmacy and GAMCC.

---

### 5.6 Testing and Quality Standards
**Issue:** Testing standards for Georgia's limited program still evolving.

**Penumbral Concern:**
- Limited testing infrastructure in-state
- Standards may differ from mature markets
- Remediation procedures for failed products
- Patient safety during quality system development

**Operator Guidance:** Exceed minimum testing standards; document quality protocols comprehensively.

## 7. Operator Best Practices

### For Licensed Operators
1. **Exceed compliance minimums** — Demonstrate commitment to restricted market integrity
2. **Document everything** — Paper trail critical in nascent program
3. **Engage GAMCC proactively** — Relationships matter in small market
4. **Patient education focus** — Program understanding reduces compliance issues

### For Pharmacies
1. **Dual compliance framework** — Board of Pharmacy + cannabis regulations
2. **Staff training comprehensive** — Unique product category
3. **Inventory integration** — Track cannabis separately with full documentation
4. **Patient verification protocols** — Registry confirmation for every transaction

### For Patients/Caregivers
1. **Maintain valid registration** — Card must be current and accessible
2. **Know possession limits** — 20 fluid ounces maximum
3. **Understand form restrictions** — Only oil in approved formats
4. **Document medical necessity** — Maintain physician relationship

### Regulatory Relationship Management
1. **Attend GAMCC meetings** — Small program means high visibility
2. **Report issues constructively** — Help shape program development
3. **Network with other licensees** — Limited peer group means mutual support value
4. **Prepare for legislative sessions** — Advocacy shapes program future
